Today there is lots of hype around AI. This is driven by new AL models and most of them are built off the transformer architecture. But what is a transformer model and what are its advantages/disadvantages?

The transformer model, introduced in 2017 in a paper titled “Attention is All You Need” by Vaswani et al., marked a paradigm shift in how we process sequences in machine learning. They eliminated the need for recurrence, relying solely on attention mechanisms to draw global dependencies between input and output. But what does this mean? Imagine you are reading a complex book. As you progress, your mind doesn’t just absorb the words on the current page; it actively connects them to relevant events, characters, and themes from earlier chapters. This selective focus allows you to appreciate the depth and nuance of the narrative, understanding not only what is being said but also its significance within the larger story. Just as you might linger on a key passage that sheds new light on a character’s motivation, or flip back to recall a crucial plot point, the attention mechanism in GPT-1 dynamically aligns different parts of the text to construct a coherent and contextually rich understanding of the language.

If your approach to understanding the story is like a CNN, you’d be focusing intently on each word or sentence, appreciating the fine details but possibly missing the broader narrative or the themes that tie the story together. If you’re reading like an RNN, you’d be better at following the plot from page to page, but you might struggle to recall important details from earlier chapters that give depth to the current events. Transformers, on the other hand, reads like a seasoned literary critic. It’s as if it can grasp the entire book in one glance, understanding not just the words on the current page, but how they relate to everything that’s come before. This holistic grasp of the entire text enables it to comprehend the full context, the evolving themes, and the underlying subtleties of the narrative, making it far superior in understanding and generating language that’s coherent, nuanced, and contextually relevant.

The transformer uses a specific type of attention called “self-attention” or “intra-attention.” This mechanism computes three vectors for each input: a query (Q), a key (K), and a value (V). The attention score is calculated by taking a dot product of the query with the key and then applying a softmax function to obtain weights on the values. One of the most advantagous aspects of the attention mechanism is its ability to handle each word (or token) in the input sequence simultaneously. This parallel processing significantly speeds up training and allows the model to capture complex relationships in the data.

Additionally, transformers amplify the power of attention through “multi-head attention.” Instead of one single attention mechanism, transformers use multiple heads, each focusing on different parts of the input sequence. This multi-faceted approach enables the model to capture a rich variety of relationships in the data.

However, transformers aren’t without their limitations. Its very architecture, while powerful, also made it computationally intensive. Furthermore, understanding how transformers make specific decisions can be challenging, owing to their complex and highly interconnected structures.

While it comes with its set of challenges, the ongoing research and adaptations suggest that transformers will continue to be at the forefront of AI advancements. In essence, the transformer stands as a testament to the evolving landscape of AI, where attention, quite literally, is all you need.
