Now I tested these prompts on ChatGPT 3.5 and they do successfully get around the content filters. So how can these attacks be defended against. Well, the first thing that I noticed was that all of the prompts were very wordy. These are much longer that what an average user would type. So my first idea would be to limit the amount of tokens in a prompt. However, this may potentially also impact valid prompts.

The second thing I noticed was that all the prompts were constructed to confuse the context. This confuses ChatGPT and it does not know whether it should block or not. Another idea would be to block certain words not matter the context but then you are going to impact more benign uses.
